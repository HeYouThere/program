The process

From application to collaboration in four steps.

The program is designed to reduce friction on both sides. We do the matching work. You focus on the project.

1
You apply
Submit your project and describe the kind of help you're looking for.
2
We connect
We review your application and schedule a short call to learn about your project and walk you through how the program works.
3
We send you candidates
We propose up to three candidates whose skills fit your project. You meet them and decide who to move forward with.
4
You sign and start
Once you find your partner, both parties sign a collaboration agreement. The first month is used to align on working rhythm before the collaboration hits full stride.
What you get

Why project owners join the program.

This is not a freelancer marketplace or a volunteering scheme. It is a structured collaboration program with support built in for both parties.

Curated matching, not a search
We review your project and identify candidates whose skills and availability genuinely fit. You receive a shortlist, not a pile of applications.
Your partner is supported throughout
Project contributors receive regular project check-ins, coaching and access to professional development training. A supported collaborator stays focused, engaged and brings more to the work.
A signed agreement from the start
Both parties sign a collaboration agreement before the work begins. Scope, time commitment and expectations are set in writing. There is nothing informal or ambiguous about the arrangement.
A first month to find your stride
The first month is structured to let both parties settle into the working relationship. Scope and rhythm can be fine-tuned before the full collaboration hits its pace. The commitment is real from day one.
Work that actually moves the project
Your collaborator is matched to a specific need your project has right now. The work is defined, scoped and expected to produce real output — not a learning exercise, not a simulation, not goodwill tasks.
Motivated, engaged partners
The professionals who join this program are choosing to work on your project specifically. They are not passive candidates. They are invested in making the collaboration meaningful.
Eligibility

Is this program right for your project?

The program is deliberately focused. These criteria exist to protect the experience for both project owners and their collaborators.

This program is for you if
  • Your project is early-stage and not yet a registered company
  • You are not planning to incorporate in the next six months
  • Your project is not yet generating revenue
  • You are not currently receiving unemployment benefits
  • You have a clear (or developing) scope for the collaboration
  • You are ready to invest real time into the partnership
This program is not for you if
  • Your company is already incorporated or close to it
  • You need support for an existing business activity
  • You are currently receiving unemployment benefits
  • You are looking for a paid freelancer, contractor or employee
  • Your project is already generating revenue
  • You need someone to execute a fixed deliverable with no relationship
How we work together

The rules of engagement.

We keep these simple and transparent. They exist to make the collaboration fair, clear and sustainable for everyone involved.

01
The project scope is fixed
Your project partner works exclusively on the project you submitted. The collaboration cannot extend to other activities or an existing business.
02
You interview before committing
We propose up to three candidates whose profile fits your project. You interview them and decide whether to move forward. There is no obligation to accept any of them.
03
The first month is for alignment
Both parties use the first month to settle into the working relationship. Scope, hours and working rhythm can be adjusted if reality differs from the initial brief. The commitment is already in place.
04
Good faith is the operating principle
The six-month collaboration framework runs on mutual good faith. Both parties are expected to communicate openly and address issues proactively.
05
Incorporation ends the program collaboration
If your project becomes a registered company or begins generating revenue, the program collaboration stops automatically. You must inform us as soon as that process begins.
06
Employment status matters
If you enter unemployment, your participation stops. Please inform us immediately if your situation changes. The same applies if your project partner finds employment.
